The InSYDE Scoop: Examining Student Comfort in Common Spaces

It is often difficult to understand how a student experiences their environment when you are not sitting in class next to them. This data-driven approach attempts to shine a light on student comfort through a series of data visualizations and key highlights to summarize the learning conditions of SYDE students in commonly used areas on campus.

% of time spent in temperature ranges


* AR stands for acceptable range. For temperature the acceptable range is based on the ASHRAE standards for building comfort.

Temperature Summary:

E5-6008:

Students spent 85% of their total recorded time in this classroom. 4.25% of their total recorded time was spent in this classroom and outside of the commonly accepted range for a comfortable building temperature.

SYDE Lounge:

Students spent 10% of their total recorded time in the SYDE Lounge, primarily to eat lunch between classes. They spent 0.5% of their total recorded time in this location and outside of the commonly accepted range for a comfortable building temperature.

DC Library:

The DC Library is a popular space for students to study outside of class, with 5% of their recorded time spent there. 0.25% of their total recorded time was spent in this location and outside of the commonly accepted range for a comfortable building temperature.
